# Voice Guide — reverse-engineered from the current resume

Anything drafted in this workshop must match this. The current resume's bullets are
already strong; the job is to match the voice, not "improve" it into something generic.

## The bullet formula

```
[Strong past-tense verb] + [object/what], [gerund phrase: the HOW / method / mechanism], [quantified outcome clause]
```

Worked examples from the live resume:

- **Operationalized** seat-overage revenue capture, **defining screening criteria across
  product usage, account health, and renewal timing**, *routing $1M+ in collectible
  expansion ARR to AEs as prioritized upsell plays*
- **Analyzed** impact of shifting enterprise renewals onto AEs **by isolating effects on
  net-new conversion, win rates, and cycle time**, *surfacing declining funnel
  productivity that drove leadership to return renewals to dedicated managers*
- **Drove** $65M divestiture of a flagship asset, **leading due diligence, financial
  modeling, and positioning strategy**, *to achieve a record-setting valuation and 120%
  return on equity*

## Rules observed in the existing copy

1. **One sentence, ~2 lines, ~190 chars.** Dense but never run-on.
2. **Lead verb is specific and active** — Operationalized, Spearheaded, Directed, Drove,
   Converted, Isolated. Not "Responsible for / Helped / Worked on."
3. **The HOW is always shown** — the middle gerund clause names the actual method
   (MaxDiff, isolating effects, defining screening criteria). This is the credibility engine.
4. **Numbers sit mid-sentence**, attached to the outcome, not stapled on at the end.
5. **No em dashes.** Commas do the joining. (Matches the repo's hard rule.)
6. **No filler adjectives** — "robust," "comprehensive," "innovative" don't appear. Good.
7. **Outcome is a business consequence**, not an activity. "drove leadership to return
   renewals," not "presented findings."

## Where the resume is already winning

The verbs and method-clauses are tight. Do not touch the style. The opportunity is in
**which stories appear** and **whether the number proves SaaS-strategy impact** — not in
sentence mechanics.
